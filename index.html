About Me

I’m a Telecommunications engineer conducting my Ph.D. in computational neuroimaging. My research is focused on multimodal brain connectivity in health and disease. I was born in Granada and after graduating from the University of Granada in 2015, I decided to move to Madrid to work as a junior programmer in technology consulting. After two years of working on projects related to treasury, I decided to start the Master in Biomedical Engineering at the University of the Basque Country which was more related to my interests. I conducted my master’s thesis in the Computational Neuroimaging Lab at Biocruces-Bizkaia HRI in 2018, and right after, I enrolled in the Biomedical Research Doctorate Program at the University of the Basque Country and started my Ph.D. in the lab. Currently, I’m up to finish it and ready to start new projects.
